- Using a piece of parchment paper as a work area, divide dough into three parts. Roll parts into three long, skinny snakes, approximately 2' long. - When fully cooked, transfer to wire cooling rack to fully cool.

Maintaining a sourdough starter:
- If not using the starter for a recipe, consider discarding ½ of the existing starter, so that it does not grow to an unmanageable size.
- Feed your starter whenever you have not used it for 2 weeks.
- In a bowl, mix equal parts flour (any variety) and water into the pre-existing sourdough starter. If needed, add a pinch of sugar or a heaping tablespoon of plain yogurt to reinvigorate starter.
- Wash out the starter container with soap and hot water, or run through the dishwasher, to get rid of existing bacteria. Return the newly-fed starter to the clean container, covering with cheesecloth held in place with a rubber band.
- Let starter stand at room temperature, stirring several times a day, until bubbly, overnight to 2 days. Do not leave at room temperature for more than 5 days without feeding.
